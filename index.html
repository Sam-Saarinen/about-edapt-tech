In addition to our existing technologies (read more below), we offer consulting services in education. We specialize in applying research-backed pedagogical principles and computational technology to novel educational problems.

quizi.us is a tool for generating and administering automatically graded assessments made of both student and instructor written questions and answers. These assessments are adaptive, only asking questions that the system estimates will be most useful for each student. quizi.us also gives detailed student and class-level insights to the instructor and makes specific pedagogical recommendations.

study.coach is an service that makes studying more effective. It combines scientifically proven techniques like spaced repetition, interleaved practice, multi-sensory encoding, and active recall to give fun and focused studying in only 5 minutes. It personalizes each learner's experience so that they learn more, faster. Teachers can use study.coach to automatically create micro-assignments involving vocabulary, problem-solving, or concept application, and it can report at any time which students know what material.

Consulting, Research, and Software for Data-Driven Education

 Case Study: FinLitRI.org Assessment conception to adoption in 5 months.

Edapt Technologies created and deployed FinLitRI.org, an adaptive assessment platform that measures proficiency in financial literacy relative to Rhode-Island adopted standards. The platform is officially recommended by the Rhode Island Department of Education for proficiency assessment in their Financial Literacy Proficiency Implementation Guide, and is now being used by schools in Rhode Island and by several non-profits with national reach.

Our company was ideally suited to respond to the need for this assessment due to our ability to rapidly generate and validate efficient adaptive assessments for new content. The legislation requiring financial literacy education and proficiency assessment in the state was signed into law in June 2021, and requires schools to begin offering financial literacy education during the 2022-2023 school year. The official standards were only adopted in December 2021, leaving only a short window of time for an assessment solution to be created that could be used to evaluate educational offerings the following academic year. Edapt Technologies leveraged our quizi.us platform to create FinLitRI.org, an adaptive assessment platform with statistically-validated and standards-aligned assessment items. By leveraging our continual validation and item-sourcing technologies, we were able to fill a critical gap in the research and infrastructure for financial literacy education. For this work, our company was recognized by the State Treasurer, the Chartered Financial Analyst Society Providence (CFA Society Providence), Financial Planners Association Rhode Island (FPA-RI), and the Rhode Island Department of Education.

FinLitRI.org is now being deployed in classrooms and by several nonprofits with national reach in order to collect data that can help increase educational efficacy over the coming year.
